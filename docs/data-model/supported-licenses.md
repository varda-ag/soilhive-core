## 7. List of Supported Licenses

Below is the list of supported licenses, each with a brief description and a link to the original documentation. If the license you need isn't listed, you can select **Other** and provide a name and a link to the relevant documentation.

| License | Description | Documentation |
|---|---|---|
| **CC0 1.0** — No Rights Reserved | The most permissive option. The rights holder waives all copyright and related rights, placing the work as close to the public domain as possible. No attribution required. | [Link](https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/) |
| **CC BY 3.0** — Attribution | Free to share and adapt for any purpose, including commercially, as long as appropriate credit is given to the original creator. | [Link](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/3.0/) |
| **CC BY 4.0** — Attribution | The current and most widely used open license. Free to share and adapt for any purpose with attribution. Recommended for maximum interoperability. | [Link](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/) |
| **CC BY-NC 3.0** — Attribution-NonCommercial | Free to share and adapt with attribution, but commercial use is not permitted. | [Link](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/3.0/) |
| **CC BY-NC 4.0** — Attribution-NonCommercial | Updated version of CC BY-NC. Free to share and adapt with attribution, excluding commercial use. | [Link](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/) |
| **CC BY-NC-SA 3.0 IGO** — Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike | Designed for intergovernmental organisations. Permits sharing and adaptation with attribution, but prohibits commercial use and requires derivative works to carry the same license. | [Link](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-sa/3.0/igo/) |
| **CC BY-SA 4.0** — Attribution-ShareAlike | Free to share and adapt with attribution. Derivative works must be distributed under the same license — a copyleft approach. | [Link](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/) |
| **ODbL v1.0** — Open Database License | Designed specifically for databases. Free to share, modify, and use the database, provided that any adapted version is released under the same license and attribution is given. | [Link](https://opendatacommons.org/licenses/odbl/1-0/) |
| **OGL-UK-3.0** — Open Government Licence | Issued by the UK Government. Free to copy, publish, and adapt the data for commercial and non-commercial purposes with attribution. | [Link](https://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/doc/open-government-licence/version/3/) |
| **LUCAS Soil Data License** | Specific license governing use of soil data from the LUCAS survey (Land Use and Coverage Area frame Survey), managed by the European Commission's Joint Research Centre. | [Link](https://esdac.jrc.ec.europa.eu/projects/LUCAS/Documents/LUCAS_SOIL_LIC_AGR_final_for_web.pdf) |
| **US Public Domain** | Data produced by the US federal government and not subject to copyright. Free to use, share, and adapt without restriction or attribution requirement. | [Link](https://www.idmanagement.gov/license/) |
